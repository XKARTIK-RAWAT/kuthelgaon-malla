About Kuthel Gaon

Kuthel Gaon is a serene Himalayan village surrounded by lush greenery, hills, and rivers. The village is known for its peaceful environment, breathtaking landscapes, and traditional architecture.

The locals are warm and hospitable, preserving their culture through festivals, music, and handicrafts. Kuthel Gaon is perfect for nature lovers, trekkers, and anyone seeking tranquility.

Village Highlights:

  • Traditional Himalayan houses
  • Terraced fields and organic farming
  • Scenic trekking trails
  • Crystal clear streams and waterfalls
  • Rich flora and fauna
  • Local cuisine specialties
  • Folk music and dance events
  • Festivals celebrating local culture

Local Cuisine

Experience the authentic flavors of Pauri Garhwal. Kuthel Gaon offers a variety of traditional dishes made from organic ingredients and age-old recipes.

Kafuli

A green leafy curry, rich in taste and nutrients.

Chainsoo

Delicious black gram lentil dish, a village specialty.

Bhang Ki Chutney

Spicy chutney made from hemp seeds and local spices.

Phaanu

Mixed lentil dish cooked in traditional clay pot.

Mandua Roti

Gluten-free flatbread made from finger millet flour.

Aloo Ke Gutke

Spicy sautéed potatoes, perfect with roti or rice.

Gahat Ki Dal

Pigeon pea lentil curry, a staple dish of the village.

Singhade Ke Pakode

Water chestnut fritters, crispy and flavorful.

Ras Bhaat

Traditional rice dish served with lentil soup and local spices.

Jhangora Roti

Flatbread made from buckwheat flour, ideal for festivals.

Culture & Heritage

Kuthel Gaon is rich in culture and traditions. The village celebrates folk music, dances, and festivals that reflect its Himalayan heritage.

Traditional Music & Dance:

  • Rai, Hurka, and Langvir folk songs
  • Choliya dance performed during festivals
  • Local musical instruments: Dhol, Nagara, Damau
  • Folk storytelling evenings in village square
  • Community singing and musical gatherings

Festivals & Events:

  • Harela Festival – celebrating harvest
  • Uttarayani Festival – honoring local traditions
  • Village Melas – handicrafts, cuisine, performances
  • Seasonal fairs during monsoon and winter
  • Harvest celebrations and communal feasts

Handicrafts & Art:

  • Wood carving and bamboo crafts
  • Handmade woolen shawls and blankets
  • Local pottery and clay utensils
  • Traditional painting and motifs in houses
  • Weaving and embroidery workshops
